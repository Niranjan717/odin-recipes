Okonomiyaki

Okonomiyaki is a savory Japanese pancake filled with a variety of ingredients. It is often referred to as "Japanese pizza" due to its customizable nature. The name translates to "grilled as you like it," reflecting the dish's versatility.

Ingredients

  • 200g all-purpose flour
  • 150ml dashi stock (or water)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 100g shredded cabbage
  • 50g green onions, chopped
  • 100g cooked shrimp or pork belly, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons pickled ginger, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Okonomiyaki sauce (for drizzling)
  • Kewpie mayonnaise (for drizzling)
  • Bonito flakes (for garnish)
  • Seaweed flakes (aonori, for garnish)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and dashi stock to create a smooth batter.
  2. Beat in the eggs until well combined.
  3. Add the shredded cabbage, green onions, cooked shrimp or pork belly, pickled ginger, salt, and pepper. Mix until all ingredients are evenly coated with the batter.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and add a tablespoon of vegetable oil.
  5. Pour a portion of the batter onto the skillet, shaping it into a round pancake about 1 inch thick. Cook for about 3-4 minutes until the bottom is golden brown.
  6. Carefully flip the pancake and cook for another 3-4 minutes on the other side until cooked through and golden brown.
  7. Repeat with the remaining batter, adding more oil to the skillet as needed.
  8. Once all pancakes are cooked, drizzle with okonomiyaki sauce and Kewpie mayonnaise in a crisscross pattern.
  9. Sprinkle with bonito flakes and seaweed flakes (aonori) for garnish.
  10. Serve hot and enjoy your homemade Okonomiyaki!

Notes

Okonomiyaki can be customized with various ingredients such as cheese, mushrooms, or even kimchi. Feel free to experiment with different toppings and fillings to suit your taste. It's a fun dish to make with friends and family, allowing everyone to create their own unique version.
